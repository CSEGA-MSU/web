How do I get a key to the Engineering Building?

Access to the Engineering Building beyond normal operating hours is granted via swipe card. Graduate students are not given after-hours access to the Engineering Building by default, but students can acquire it if needed. To activate your MSU ID for access to the Engineering Building, follow these steps. First, explain to your faculty advisor why you require access. Then, your adviser will notify the CSE secretary of your need. the CSE secretary will, in turn, process your request to have access to the Engineering Building added to your ID. To expedite your request, please have your advisor include your PID and proxy number (the number following the * on the back of your MSU ID) with the request.

Access to labs and offices is by key. You may request a key to your lab or office through your advisor as well. The process is the same. You will make a request to your advisor who will then submit the request to the the CSE secretary, who will in turn issue you a key. Finally, pick up your key in the CSE office. You must return your key to prior to graduation, or the department will place a hold on your degree.

How do I access on-line resources like the ACM Digital Library and IEEE Xplore when I'm off campus?

You can access these resources using the library's proxy server. The easiest way to use the proxy is to simply search for materials via the MSU Library's Electronic Resources page. If you use a firewall, you will need to allow outgoing connections on ports 2047 and 443.

Where do I go for registration/class/account/calendar information?

StuInfo has information about academic, financial balances addendance, contact information, and more. Payroll handles your salaries. The Registrar handles enroolment. registration, and calendars. And the MSU Schedule of Courses has the current course offereings.

Where are the forms I need to fill out each year?

The Graduate Tracking System has most of the CSE forms you'll need to fill out for the year. The CSE Graduate Database has your SIRS forms. The Graduate Reporting System (GRS) has your annual progress report. Research Training Tracking System (RTTS) is where you need to report your ethics (RCR) training each year.

What email addresses are provided to me?

CSE graduate students are given three email address, and email can (and will) be sent to any of them. Mail forwarding is your friend. The email login pages are: MSU Webmail, CSE Webmail, and Engineering Webmail.

Where do I print posters or find a 3D printer?

For poster printing and 3D printing go to DECS - Division of Engineering Computing Services on the first floor of the EB. Posters are 200 pages out of your print quota or $10 (as of 2015). It costs about $1 per 10 grams (as of 2015).

Where do I go for technical help (classroom problems, off campus program access like MATLAB) ?

The Computer Facility page has information for computer science lab support (3rd Floor EB computer labs). If you are really stuck ask the Systems Staff. For general classroom problems us the IT Services Support Desk. For other EB computer labs problems and off campus application access go to DECS - Division of Engineering Computing Services.
